# Reflection

#How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?

Interpreting user needs and turning them into a program starts with understanding what the user needs and wants to accomplish. Breaking what they need into smaller, clear requiremtns makes implementation easier. Creating user stories helps becayse they keep everything focused on the user's perspective. Using the format makes the goal and purpose clear, which supports guide development and testing.

#How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

When developing programs, I break the work into smaller pieces instead of trying to do everyting at once. Agile processes like working in interations, getting feedback early, and adjusting as needed help avoid bigger issues down the line. I plant to continue using practices like sprint planning, regular check ins, and continuous improvement because they keep work organized while staying flexible.

#What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good team member in software development means communicating clearly, being reliable, and being open to feedback. It's important to stay aligned with the team, help others, and adapt when priorities change. A strong team member focuses not only on their own tasks but also on the overall success of the team.
